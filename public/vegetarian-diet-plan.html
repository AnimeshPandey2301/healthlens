🚫 Caution

Foods & Habits to Avoid

Common pitfalls in vegetarian diets that can undermine your health goals.

🍬
Excess Refined Sugar
White sugar, sweets, and sodas cause inflammation, insulin spikes, and displace nutrient-dense foods.
🍟
Ultra-Processed Snacks
Chips, packaged biscuits, and instant noodles are high in trans fats, sodium, and empty calories.
☕
Excess Caffeine & Tea
More than 2 cups/day can inhibit iron and calcium absorption — space meals & hot drinks apart.
🧂
High Sodium Foods
Pickles, papadums, and salty snacks raise blood pressure and increase calcium excretion.
🥤
Sugary Beverages
Fruit juices with added sugar, energy drinks, and aerated drinks contribute to obesity and dental decay.
🍞
Excess Refined Grains
White bread and maida (all-purpose flour) lack fibre and spike blood sugar. Opt for whole grains.
✨ Pro Tips

Optimize Your Vegetarian Diet

Simple, science-backed strategies to get the most from plant-based eating.

🌞
Vitamin D Sunlight & Supplements
Spend 15–20 min in morning sun and consider a Vitamin D3 supplement — most vegetarians are deficient.
💊
Supplement B12 Religiously
Vitamin B12 is found almost exclusively in animal products. All vegetarians should supplement 500–1000 mcg weekly.
🫘
Soak & Sprout Your Legumes
Soaking reduces phytic acid, increasing the absorption of iron, zinc, and calcium from beans and lentils.
🍋
Pair Iron With Vitamin C
Non-haem iron from plants is less bioavailable. Add lemon juice or tomatoes to iron-rich meals to boost uptake.
💧
Hydrate Smartly
Aim for 8–10 glasses of water daily. High-fibre vegetarian diets need more fluids to aid digestion and prevent constipation.
🥜
Diversify Your Protein Sources
Rotate between dal, paneer, tofu, beans, quinoa, and nuts to ensure a complete amino acid profile throughout the week.
